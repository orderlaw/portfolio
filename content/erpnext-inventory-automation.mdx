# ERPNext Inventory Automation

### Overview

A retail operation running three physical locations had a simple problem: nobody agreed on what was in stock. Each location tracked inventory in its own spreadsheet, purchase orders were raised manually when someone noticed a shelf was low, and stock counts at month-end regularly came back wrong.

They came to me six weeks before peak season.

---

## The Problem

The business was losing money in two directions simultaneously. Overstock on slow items tied up cash, while popular lines ran out mid-week because reorders happened reactively — someone noticed a gap and raised a ticket. By the time stock arrived, the window was gone.

The root cause wasn't discipline. It was that the system required humans to act as the connective tissue between three stores and a central warehouse. Any absence, distraction, or miscommunication broke the chain.

**What they needed:**

- A single view of stock across all three locations
- Automatic reorder triggers when items hit their minimum threshold
- Purchase orders that generated themselves and routed to the right supplier
- Zero manual reconciliation at month-end

---

## What Was Built

ERPNext was already in the stack but barely used — mostly for invoicing. The build extended it properly.

### Custom Doctypes

Two new doctypes handled the logic that the standard Stock Ledger couldn't express cleanly: a `Location Stock Rule` doctype tied each item-location combination to its minimum quantity and preferred supplier, and a `Reorder Log` doctype captured every trigger event for auditing.

### Scripted Reorder Triggers

A server-side script runs after every stock movement. When an item at any location drops below its minimum, the script checks whether an open purchase order for that item already exists. If not, it creates one and routes it to the supplier defined in the Location Stock Rule.

No duplicate orders. No missed triggers. No manual intervention.

### Cross-Location Stock View

A custom report surfaces live stock across all three locations in a single view, with colour-coded thresholds and drill-down to movement history. The ops team replaced four spreadsheets with one tab.

---

## Results

- Stock discrepancies at month-end dropped from an average of **12%** to under **1%** within the first month
- Manual purchase orders eliminated entirely — the system creates and routes them automatically
- Reorder lead time reduced by **3 days** on average because orders go out the moment threshold is hit, not when someone notices
- Peak season ran without a single stockout on tracked items
