# WooCommerce + n8n Integration

### Overview

An e-commerce business processing a few hundred orders a week had a fulfilment team spending four hours a day on tasks that should have taken zero. Every order touched by a human was an order that could be delayed, misrouted, or forgotten.

They wanted the humans back on the floor, not in front of spreadsheets.

---

## The Problem

The existing process looked like this: order comes in via WooCommerce, someone checks it manually, assigns it to a warehouse pick list, emails a dispatch note, updates the order status, and logs it in a separate tracking sheet. Then they checked again in the afternoon to see if anything had shipped.

Every single one of those steps was a hand-off that could go wrong. And at volume, some did, every day — missing status updates, duplicated picks, customers emailing support about orders that had already shipped.

**The failure points:**

- No automatic status updates between WooCommerce and the warehouse system
- Dispatch confirmations handled by email, not data
- Support tickets generated by information lag, not actual fulfilment problems

---

## What Was Built

n8n sits in the middle. It listens, decides, and acts — without anyone watching.

### Order Intake Workflow

A webhook fires on every new WooCommerce order. n8n validates the payload, checks inventory availability, and routes the order to the correct warehouse queue. Orders with special handling requirements (fragile items, combined shipments, oversized) follow a separate branch with additional validation steps before queuing.

### Status Update Pipeline

When the warehouse system marks a pick as complete, n8n catches the outbound webhook, maps the status to WooCommerce's order schema, and updates the order automatically. The customer gets their dispatch email within seconds of the pick being confirmed — not when someone remembers to send it.

### Fulfilment Triggers

Payment failures, address validation errors, and out-of-stock situations each have their own n8n branch. Failures route to a dedicated Slack channel with context. The support team sees the problem before the customer emails.

### Tracking Sheet Elimination

The separate tracking spreadsheet was replaced by a Supabase table that n8n writes to on every state change. The ops team now has a live view rather than a manually-maintained log.

---

## Results

- **4 hours/day** of manual processing eliminated across the fulfilment team
- Customer-facing dispatch notifications now go out **automatically**, within seconds of pick completion
- Support tickets related to order status dropped by **~70%** in the first month
- Zero missed orders since go-live — every edge case has a defined branch
